## 3. Reproduction evidence — the FAIL

Two independent reproduction checks, both **FAIL**:

| Check | Result | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Prefix vs run 1** (first 10 rows re-run) | **9/10** identical — battle `seed_index 9` DIFFERS | `data/eval/t4/repro-prefix.txt` |
| **Run 1 vs run 2** (full 51 re-run) | **48/51** identical — diverging `seed_index {9, 19, 48}` | `data/eval/t4/repro-run1-vs-run2.txt` |

Across the full double-run, the coarse row metrics agree while the byte streams do not:
**winners 51/51 match**, **seeds 51/51 match**, **turns 50/51** (only row 9 differs: **13 vs 11**).
So the divergence is real battle-trajectory nondeterminism, not a metadata artifact — and it is
invisible to winner/seed checks alone, which is exactly why a byte-level reproduction gate exists.

The three divergent battles (all forced post-faint replacements):

| seed_index | cell (opp_policy / team) | divergence point |
|---|---|---|
| 9  | `heuristic` / `trickroom_dev`     | **villain** double replacement — p2 `\|switch\|` order flips (Hatterene/Amoonguss) |
| 19 | `greedy_protect` / `trickroom_dev`| **hero** replacement — p1b Rillaboom vs Flutter Mane slot flips |
| 48 | `greedy_protect` / `rain_dev`     | **hero** replacement — p1b Flutter Mane vs Tornadus slot flips |

Diffs: `repro-run2-battle{9,19,48}-diff.txt`, `repro-prefix-battle9-diff.txt`. Every diff's first
changed line is a `|switch|` immediately after a `forceSwitch` request — the choice of **which mon
goes to which slot / in which order** is what flips.

**Triangulation → binary-choice nondeterminism.** For `seed_index 9`, the **prefix** battle-9 log
and the **run-2** battle-9 log are **byte-identical to each other**, and **run 1** is the outlier.
Two of three processes independently landed on the same replacement, the third on the other — the
signature of an unseeded coin flip over a 2-option choice, not a data-dependent bug.

**Consequence:** rows containing such battles are **not pairable** across runs (their byte streams
and even turn counts differ run-to-run). Under T5's paired design this is a **T5-blocking defect
class** — a battle whose trajectory changes between the baseline run and the candidate run cannot
enter a McNemar pair. Catching this before T5/T6 is precisely the purpose of the reproduction gate.

## 4. Root cause (verified in code)

The client's forced-replacement path falls through to an **unseeded RNG**:

1. A pure `forceSwitch` request yields no joint move-actions, so the heuristic raises
   `ValueError("no legal joint actions")` at
   `showdown_bot/src/showdown_bot/battle/decision.py:233` (`enumerate_my_actions` is empty).
2. `choose_with_fallback` catches it (`decision.py:619`) and tries `max_damage_choice`
   (`decision.py:627`), which also fails on a force-switch (no damaging move to pick) → logs
   `max_damage fallback failed` (`decision.py:629`).
3. It then falls to `pick_random_pair(req)` (`decision.py:632`).
4. `pick_random_pair` defaults its rng to a fresh **unseeded** `random.Random()` at
   `showdown_bot/src/showdown_bot/battle/random_agent.py:11` → the replacement slot/order is
   **nondeterministic per process**.

**Log correlation (run 1 `client.log`).** 8 `no legal joint actions` warnings total:
- **4 mid-battle** cases with **no** subsequent fallback-failure line → the random fallback
  **succeeded** and silently returned a nondeterministic replacement. These are the bug. They
  match the **3 divergent battles** plus **1 coincidental** case (both processes happened to pick
  the same option — the benign side of a coin flip).
- **4 end-of-battle** cases where all three fallbacks fail (`max_damage` + `random` both log a
  failure) → the benign already-decided cascade documented in the T3f report; no gate impact.

**Why T1b/T1c/T3f never caught it.** Those smokes were mirror / short battles with **no
mid-battle forced replacements**, so this path was only ever hit at end-of-battle (all-cascade,
benign) or not at all. T4 at scale — 51 games, real dev-team opponents, longer battles with
faints — is the **first** to drive a mid-battle forced replacement through the fallback. Surfacing
exactly this class of defect is what T4 is for.

## 8. Follow-up (named, not implemented)

A small **dedicated fix slice**: **deterministic forced-replacement handling in the client
decision path**. The heuristic should produce a deterministic switch-in on `forceSwitch` requests;
at minimum the last-resort fallback (`pick_random_pair`) must be **deterministic / seeded** rather
than defaulting to an unseeded `random.Random()`. This touches `battle/` → **INV-1** applies, so it
needs its **own plan + review + user sign-off** before any code change.

After the fix, a full T4 re-run (~10 min) must produce **10/10 prefix identity AND 51/51
double-run identity**. **That** run's artifacts become the T5 fixture — **this** run's committed
data is evidence, not fixture.
